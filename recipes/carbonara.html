Fettuccine Carbonara

Description

Fettuccine Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish that is beloved for its rich, creamy, and indulgent flavors. This timeless recipe originated in the Lazio region of Italy and has since gained popularity worldwide. Traditionally, it consists of cooked fettuccine noodles coated in a velvety sauce made with eggs, Parmesan cheese, crispy pancetta or bacon, black pepper, and sometimes a touch of cream. The hot pasta cooks the eggs slightly, creating a luscious, silky texture that beautifully clings to each strand.

The result is a harmonious blend of savory and salty flavors, with a hint of warmth from the black pepper. Fettuccine Carbonara is a true comfort food that never fails to satisfy pasta lovers seeking a satisfying and unforgettable dining experience.

Ingredients

The required ingredients for this recipe include:

  • 8 ounces (225 grams) of fettuccine pasta
  • 4 ounces (113 grams) of pancetta or bacon, diced into small pieces
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup (100 grams) of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon of freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste (optional)
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

Directions

To cook this delicious meal, you can follow these steps:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the fettuccine pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain the cooked pasta, reserving some of the pasta water.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced pancetta or bacon and cook until it becomes crispy and browned. Remove the cooked pancetta from the skillet and set it aside on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess grease.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Parmesan cheese, and freshly ground black pepper until well combined.
  4. Reduce the heat to low and return the cooked pasta to the skillet with the pancetta. Pour the egg mixture over the pasta, tossing quickly and continuously to coat the pasta evenly. The heat from the pasta will gently cook the eggs, creating a creamy sauce. If the mixture seems too thick, you can gradually add a small amount of the reserved pasta water to loosen it.
  5. Once the pasta is well coated with the sauce and heated through, remove the skillet from the heat. Be careful not to overcook the sauce, as the eggs may scramble.
  6. Taste the fettuccine carbonara and season with salt if desired. Remember that the pancetta or bacon already adds saltiness, so be cautious while adding salt.
  7. Serve the fettuccine carbonara immediately, garnished with chopped fresh parsley if desired. Enjoy the creamy and flavorful pasta while it's still hot.
